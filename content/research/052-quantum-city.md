## 1. Introduction: The Night the Network Held

### 1.0 Origin: What Kyiv Proved

This paper began not with a hurricane but with a war. When Russia invaded Ukraine in February 2022, it attacked the two things this paper is about at once: the network and the record. Yet the country stayed readable. Within a week, Kyivstar, Vodafone Ukraine, and lifecell pooled into national roaming, so a cell knocked off the air handed its users to a rival's tower rather than dropping them [demonstrated] (Mind.ua, 2023; Developing Telecoms, 2024). Operators ran base stations on batteries, generators, and fuel carried by hand under fire; on 24 November 2022, Kyivstar lost commercial grid power at 65% of its sites and kept its core alive anyway [demonstrated] (Light Reading, 2023). When sustained strikes on the electricity grid cascaded into water, heat, and telecommunications failures, the interdependency itself became the exposed vulnerability [demonstrated] (Congressional Research Service, 2024). And the state's memory survived because a February 2022 law let public data leave physical servers before missiles reached them — roughly ten million gigabytes dispersed abroad, with the Diia platform keeping services legible to citizens whose government offices had been shelled [deployed] (Tung, 2022; Nextgov, 2022), echoing Estonia's data-embassy doctrine of replicating sovereign data beyond the reach of any single crisis [deployed] (e-Estonia, 2018).

Two counter-lessons complete the picture and set this paper's axis. In December 2023, the Sandworm group did not merely disrupt Kyivstar; it wiped core IT infrastructure, taking out service for roughly 24 million subscribers and forcing a rebuild from backups rather than a restart [demonstrated] (Antoniuk, 2023). And Starlink, which activated as edge failover when terrestrial links were destroyed, also demonstrated the chokepoint of a single vendor able to geofence or withdraw service unilaterally, in violation of the resilience principle it otherwise served [demonstrated] (Chatham House, 2024). Cloud did not make data safe; on-premise did not make it sovereign. Kyiv proved that the real question is neither cloud nor on-premise but **reachability under partition**: whether the network and the record stay reachable, and under whose control, when any single location is severed. **No data should depend on one reachable place.** Everything that follows — the hurricanes, the deployments, the economics — is a variation on the lesson Kyiv taught first.

### 1.1 Inside the operations center, two futures at once

The same axis governs peacetime disasters. At 02:14 the wall of a network operations center turns from green to amber to red, cell by cell, west to east. the duty operations engineer watches a hurricane's eyewall cross the island as a moving shadow across her topology map: sites drop not because the storm has touched them yet, but because the commercial grid beneath them has failed and their batteries are counting down. She has done this before. She knows the shape of what comes next — and, this year, she knows the shape of what could come instead. Two networks share her screen. One is dying the way networks have always died. The other is being built, unevenly, one procurement clause at a time.

The network of today dies from the inside out. When Hurricane Helene made landfall in September 2024, 48.7% of cell sites in the impacted areas of western North Carolina went out of service, and most were not restored until roughly three weeks later [demonstrated] (Congressional Research Service, 2024). The radios were rarely the problem. High winds and floodwaters destroyed more than 1,700 miles of fiber-optic backhaul, so that even sites with power and functioning antennas sat mute, severed from the core [demonstrated] (Asheville Watchdog, 2025); operators reported backhaul as the binding constraint on restoration even where power and radios were fine [demonstrated] (Fierce Network, 2024). Call this the **Helene backhaul severance**: the failure is topological before it is meteorological — intelligence lives far from the point of action, and the path to it is fragile. The Iberian blackout of 28 April 2025 made the dependency total: cascading grid failure cut power to more than 50 million people for roughly ten hours across most of the peninsula, up to sixteen hours in parts of Spain, and telecommunications, rail, and emergency services failed with the grid, because comms had been built to assume the grid [demonstrated] (ENTSO-E, 2025). Call this the **Iberian partition**; both events are referenced by name hereafter rather than re-quoted.

And when the network dies, reality becomes unreadable. Helene disabled NOAA's National Centers for Environmental Information in Asheville — the national climate-data archive — through combined loss of power, internet, and cooling water, forcing improvised cooling from fire-department tankers to protect the record [demonstrated] (NC State University News, 2025). Data that described the very hazards unfolding sat trapped behind an infrastructure that the hazard had taken offline. This is the deepest failure mode of the incumbent architecture: it sends the world's recordings *away*, to clouds and regions reachable only when everything works, and so loses the world precisely when the world most needs reading.

### 1.2 The network being built

The second network on the operator's screen behaves differently, and its components are already real. Late in October 2025, when Hurricane Melissa's winds exceeding 165 mph shattered Jamaica's terrestrial networks, Liberty Caribbean activated Starlink Direct-to-Cell as a satellite-backhaul failover, so that compatible LTE phones gained a satellite link with no special equipment, no app, and no user action when the towers went offline [deployed] (Data Center Dynamics, 2025; Liberty Latin America, 2025). During Helene, AT&T's FirstNet deployed Compact Rapid Deployables that link to the core by satellite and *do not rely on commercial power* — degraded-mode restoration rehearsed in advance rather than improvised in the wreckage [deployed] (AT&T, 2024), with regulators granting emergency authority for satellite-to-phone service over the disaster zone [deployed] (RCR Wireless News, 2024). These are not the network healing itself; they are humans carrying a spare network in on trucks and satellites. But they prove the design principle the operator is watching mature: keep the readable model of reality *local and sovereign*, so that partition degrades service rather than erasing it.

Self-healing is the next rung, and here this paper contributes primary evidence. Analyzing 12,819,049 handover attempts across 75 cells in Rakuten's dense-urban Shibuya Open RAN cluster, we found six problem cells whose handover success sat near 84%; targeted Cell Individual Offset (CIO) tuning across 22 neighbor relations — a one-to-two-decibel adjustment guided by Mobility Robustness Optimization — lifted problem-cell handover success on the acted relations from approximately 84% to approximately 92%, a mean gain of +8.0 percentage points with a maximum of +11.1 points [demonstrated]. A closed control loop, acting on the operator's own live telemetry, restored the network from inside itself. That is the self-healing seed from which the disaster-grade version grows; it is developed in full, with method, in Chapter 4 and referenced by name thereafter.

One line downstream, this is why it matters: in the hospital two kilometers from the operator's console, the downstream hospital's patient-telemetry feed either holds through the night or goes silent — and whether it holds is decided by architecture choices made years earlier, in rooms she will never see.

### 1.3 The unit of contribution

This paper advances a single claim and defends it once: **reality is the primary, perpetually-recorded construct — the boundary-spanning object that operator, city, and clinic each read in their own vocabulary yet physically share — and the architecture decisions that make that recording sovereign, explainable, and quantum-safe are being locked in now.** Every dashboard, forecast, and cloud is a secondary, corrigible instrument calibrated against reality, never a substitute for it. The incumbent architecture inverts this ordering and pays for it in exactly the failures above; the chapters that follow build in the opposite direction, from reality outward, and call each established figure back by name rather than re-quoting it.

The claim is falsifiable, and the falsifier is named here and elevated throughout: the **partition test**. If a system's readable model of reality survives loss of its network and power — if operators still act, if the downstream hospital's patient-telemetry feed still resolves locally — the thesis holds; if reality goes dark the moment the cloud is unreachable, it fails. This has been *illustrated* in the field, once by accident, in our first primary case — the Vöfab/Växjö deployment (Case A) — where a real power outage forced the system into degraded, edge-local mode. That event demonstrated the partition condition is real; it was not a controlled failover trial, and §3 states plainly what a rigorous partition test would require.

### 1.4 Method, and an honest circularity

This is a structured evidence-synthesis and architecture-position paper, anchored by two primary deployment cases the author conducted: the WINNIIO Vöfab "Smart Värme" edge-native school-heating deployment in Växjö, Sweden (Case A), and the Altiostar/Rakuten Shibuya Open RAN dataset above (Case B). Sources were selected for primacy (operator and regulator records over vendor marketing, peer-reviewed theory over commentary) and every factual claim carries an explicit tier — [deployed], [demonstrated], [projection], or [target] — with the reasoning made legible rather than smoothed into confident prose.

The Smart Värme deployment supplies the partition illustration as lived fact. Six wireless sensors per room drove millimetre-precision radiator actuation with intelligence processed locally in the building; the digital twin, added on the author's initiative, became the boundary object through which technicians, teachers, and facility managers finally shared one legible picture of the heating system [deployed]. Then a real power outage struck mid-project: the actuator valves closed, the school went cold, and the fault was — initially, wrongly — blamed on the project [deployed]. Degraded-mode behavior stopped being a design slogan and became an observed event. Over four months the system cut energy use by roughly 9% against a 20% target [demonstrated] — a partial, honestly short result whose per-building decomposition (the preschool showed no measurable gain; two schools fought commissioning problems) Chapter 3 neither inflates nor buries, and which the Appendix claim ledger records as author-reported and un-audited.

The method carries two constructs the author declares plainly as artifacts under evaluation, not settled theory. The first is the author's own **SMILE** methodology (Waern, 2026), which is therefore *both* the instrument of analysis *and* a contribution to be tested. Four SMILE constructs are used sparingly and only where they do real work: **NUDEDA** (proceed from an identified need toward data, never data-first); **SPIN** (every twin addresses Space, Place, Interaction, Network); **AEST**, available at any anchor point (Absorb the past, Emulate the living present, Simulate forward, Transcend by reverse-engineering the path from a desired future back to present action); and **Phase 6, Perpetual Wisdom**, the commitment to knowledge that outlives project closure — which this paper extends to *perpetual reality recording*, and whose shadow, that durable recordings become long-horizon security liabilities the moment they are captured, Chapter 2 takes up. The second is the author-proposed **DTC (Digital-Twin Continuum) quantum-maturity spine** used in Chapters 4 and 8 — quantum-aware (QA), quantum-inspired (QI), quantum-ready (QR), hybrid quantum-classical (HQC), quantum-native (QN) — offered, exactly like SMILE, as an artifact under evaluation rather than an established external framework. Reality, digitally rendered, is the ultimate boundary object (Waern, 2026); the night the network holds is the night that object stays readable in the dark.

## 2. Method and Evidence Base

### 2.1 What this artifact is

This paper is a structured evidence-synthesis and architecture-position paper, anchored by two primary WINNIIO deployment cases. It is not a systematic review, and it does not claim exhaustive coverage of its literatures. It advances one falsifiable architectural thesis (stated in full at §1.3) and tests it against evidence assembled under an explicit, tier-labeled selection discipline. The method lens is SMILE v4.0 (Waern, 2026), applied — per that framework's own NUDEDA construct — need-first: each source and each case earns its place by answering a stated question about whether reality can remain readable, sovereign, and quantum-safe when the network degrades, rather than by data-first accumulation.

The two primary cases are the analytic spine, and the author reports both from inside the deployments — a stance declared here so the reader can weight it. The first is WINNIIO's **Vöfab/Växjö "Smart Värme" edge-native school-heating deployment** [deployed]: six sensors per room, millimetre-precision radiator actuation, and a digital twin operated as a boundary object across facilities, operations, and municipal stakeholders — during which a real power outage forced the system into degraded, edge-local mode, illustrating the partition condition. The second is the **Altiostar/Rakuten Shibuya Open RAN mobility-robustness dataset** [demonstrated]: across 75 cells and 12.8M handover attempts, self-healing via closed-loop CIO tuning lifted problem-cell handover success on the acted relations from ~84% to ~92% (mean +8.0 points, peak +11.1). The first case grounds the sovereign-edge and boundary-object claims at building scale; the second grounds the operator self-healing claim at network scale, against the peer-reviewed MRO/CIO mechanism (Nguyen & Kwon, 2018; Onireti et al., 2016) and independent O-RAN RIC results (Kitagawa et al., 2023).

### 2.2 Source-selection strategy

Selection was purposive, not exhaustive. The author sought, for each load-bearing claim, the highest-tier source reachable: a primary operator or standards artifact where one exists, a peer-reviewed result where mechanism is at stake, and a labeled projection only where no deployed evidence yet exists. Disaster evidence was restricted to documented 2023–2026 events, prioritising primary regulatory or government reporting where available — the Iberian blackout (ENTSO-E, 2025), Hurricanes Helene and Melissa (Federal Communications Commission, 2024; Congressional Research Service, 2024; Data Center Dynamics, 2025), Hurricane Beryl (Jamaica Information Service, 2024). Two events are corroborated only by secondary or encyclopedic aggregation at time of writing — the Greek wildfires (2023 Greece wildfires, n.d.; 2024 Greek wildfires, n.d.) and the 2026 Venezuela earthquakes (2026 Venezuela earthquakes, n.d.; U.S. Department of State, 2026) — and are labeled accordingly, with magnitudes and casualty counts marked provisional rather than carried as verified. Cryptographic claims were anchored to ratified standards (National Institute of Standards and Technology, 2024) and authoritative mandates (National Security Agency, 2022), not vendor roadmaps. Vendor, consulting, and marketing figures are admitted only when labeled as such and never carried as verified magnitudes.

### 2.3 Evidence tiers

Every substantive claim in this paper carries one of four labels. **[deployed]** — running in production and observed (Vöfab actuation; NIST FIPS 203–205 ratification). **[demonstrated]** — shown in a field trial, pilot, or documented event, but not sustained production (Shibuya CIO gains; Starlink Direct-to-Cell failover). **[projection]** — a reasoned extrapolation from deployed or demonstrated anchors, explicitly not a forecast. **[target]** — a standardized design goal or mandate not yet met (CNSA 2.0's 2035 horizon). Where a source is peer-reviewed theory rather than field evidence, it is marked **[peer-reviewed-theory]**; where a magnitude comes from a vendor or consulting secondary source, it is marked **[vendor-reported]**. The labels are load-bearing: an architecture decision justified by a [projection] carries a different procurement weight than one justified by a [deployed] result, and conflating the two — or promoting a vendor figure to verified fact — is the failure mode this method exists to prevent.

### 2.4 The falsifiable core and its failure condition

The unit of contribution is stated in full once, at §1.3, and defended throughout; this section states only its failure condition. The falsifier is the **partition test**: when the wide-area network dies, a sovereign edge-native node must keep reality locally readable and actionable, and rehearsed failover must restore cross-domain service without central mediation. If, under real partition, the edge cannot read and act on its own recorded reality — if the twin goes dark with the link — the thesis fails. The Vöfab outage illustrates the test at building scale; the Shibuya self-healing loop is its network-scale analogue. Disaster evidence (the Iberian partition; the Helene backhaul severance) supplies the counterfactual the thesis must beat.

### 2.5 Limitations of the two cases

The primary evidence is deliberately narrow, and its limits bound the claims. Both cases are single-author-adjacent and non-independently-audited; the Appendix claim ledger flags every headline number from them as author-reported. Vöfab is one municipality, one building class (schools), one climate, and one observed outage — an existence proof of degraded-mode sovereignty, not a distribution of it, and not a controlled A/B failover trial. Shibuya is one dense-urban cluster over one measurement window; critically, the raw handover *counters* are production data, but the handover-*outcome classification* rests on a synthetic-mobility watermark in the dataset rather than fully independent ground truth. Because the outcome layer is partly synthetic, the CIO gains are labeled [demonstrated] throughout — never [deployed] — and [deployed] is reserved only for the fact that the counters came from a production network. Neither case tests cross-administrative-domain intent negotiation at city scale, which remains a [projection] throughout. Section 10.1 consolidates these and the paper's other limitations; the claim ledger (Appendix) maps every headline number to its source and tier so the reader can audit the gap between what is shown and what is argued.

## 3. Primary Case A: The Building That Kept Its Own Mind

The tender did not ask for a digital twin. It asked for heat. When Vöfab — the property company of Växjö municipality — opened the *Smart Värme* challenge under the GodaHus and Diaccess innovation programmes, the stated need was energy saving across three schools (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]). I led the WINNIIO team that answered it. This is the NUDEDA discipline made literal: the need came first, and data was the last thing anyone wanted (Waern, 2026, DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19646240 [deployed]). Everything I describe below, I ran or watched run. I label each claim by evidence tier and I do not smooth the failures into confident prose.

**The instrument of ground truth (SPIN: Space).** We placed six wireless, battery-powered sensors in every room — temperature, CO₂, humidity, radiator return-temperature, presence, and window-open state (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]). Six streams per room is not telemetry maximalism; it is the minimum needed to distinguish a warm room from an *occupied* room from a room whose window someone propped open in February. Space, in SMILE's SPIN vocabulary, is "the objective, measurable physical environment [that] has properties that exist independently of any community's description of them" (Waern, 2026 [deployed]). The sensors are how the classroom stops being a matter of opinion. New actuators then closed the loop, opening and closing radiator valves with millimetre precision, integrated at the undercentral so the twin governed both the heat exchanger's supply temperature and each individual radiator (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]).

**The jurisdiction (SPIN: Place).** A classroom is not only a volume of air; it is a Place — "the humanly meaningful interpretation of space" where different communities negotiate around a common referent (Waern, 2026 [deployed]). A 09:00 lesson with roughly thirty pupils is, thermally, about 3000 W of body heat walking through the door — a load the twin can anticipate and offset without any temperature drop (Vöfab, 2023 [demonstrated]). The Place, not the Space, is what the schedule, the caretaker, and the pre-heat curve all argue about. This 3000 W / thirty-pupil arrival is the reference load referenced by name in later chapters.

**The twin as boundary object.** WINNIIO added something the tender never requested: a digital twin whose primary job was to be a boundary object — the shared artefact through which technicians, school staff, and facility managers could all *see* how ventilation, electricity, and VVS behaved, and where a fault actually arose (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]). This is Star and Griesemer's (1989) construct made physical: one referent, read differently by each community, requiring no shared vocabulary. Vendors could commission against a digital version of the school before touching the real building. This is the concrete instance of the thesis this paper defends — reality, digitally rendered, as the boundary-spanning object — rather than a diagram of it.

**Sovereignty as an architectural choice, not a slogan.** The gateway processed data locally in the building first; only processed information travelled onward, and only if the owner chose to send it. The owner selects: self-learning algorithms running *in-building* on the edge, or intelligence supplied from a third-party cloud (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]). That choice is the whole argument of this whitepaper compressed into one procurement decision. Edge-native is not a performance optimisation here; it is who controls the mind of the building.

**AEST on one school.** SMILE's temporal model activated at the anchor (Waern, 2026 [deployed]). *Absorb*: the twin ingested the building's history — return temperatures, occupancy rhythm, the fabric's thermal lag. *Emulate*: it held a living present-state model, six streams per room, updated continuously. *Simulate*: it ran the pre-heat forward — the 09:00 class, the reference load arriving — and tested the valve schedule virtually before committing a single degree. *Transcend*: it reverse-engineered the path from the comfort-and-savings target back to the actuator command issued now. Sensor-to-central-only products claim roughly 20% savings; room-level control is theorised up to about 30% (Vöfab, 2023 [projection — comparator figure, not a measured deployment result]).

**The honest outcomes.** I will not launder these. Over four months the portfolio cut energy use by roughly 9% against a 20% target [demonstrated] — and that aggregate hides an uneven distribution the claim ledger records: the preschool showed no measurable impact (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]), and two schools hit real initial temperature-regulation and commissioning problems — radiators not optimally controllable at first, and the communication friction of any live building integration (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]). A twin that only reported its triumphs would be a hallucination-friendly architecture, exactly what SMILE's Crucible layer forbids (Waern, 2026 [deployed]).

**The power outage as the partition illustration.** Then the grid failed. A real *strömavbrott* struck during the project and temporarily worsened the commissioning difficulties — an unplanned degraded-mode event on a live deployment (Vöfab, 2023 [deployed]). This prefigures the disaster chapters at building scale. When the power drops, a cloud-dependent design loses its mind: the intelligence lived elsewhere, the backhaul is down, and the building reverts to a dumb thermostat blind to occupancy — reality becomes unreadable the moment the network dies. An edge-sovereign design degrades differently. The in-building gateway retains the local model and the last-known schedule; the six sensors and the millimetre actuators remain a closed loop that does not require a round-trip to a datacentre to know that a window is open or a room is empty. When power returns, the twin resynchronises upward rather than rebooting from nothing.

I state the limit of this claim honestly. The Växjö outage exposed the *stakes* of the edge-versus-cloud decision on a real building; it was not instrumented as a controlled A/B failover trial, and I do not present it as one. What it demonstrates [demonstrated] is that partition is not a hypothetical for distributed building intelligence — it is a Tuesday. What a rigorous partition test would require is stated plainly: a pre-registered failover protocol, an instrumented control arm running cloud-dependent, and measured recovery-time and state-continuity metrics across both arms. What the architecture *promises* under partition [projection] is what Primary Case B and the disaster scenarios then test at network and city scale.

The building that kept its own mind is the smallest complete instance of this paper's unit of contribution: a perpetually-recorded reality, sovereign at the edge, legible to every community that gathers around it — and readable even when the wider world goes dark.

## 4. Primary Case B: Self-Healing Handovers with Real Numbers

Chapter 3 gave the operator a heartbeat that survives a power cut. This chapter asks the harder question: when the network stays up but *degrades*, can it heal itself before a human notices? The Altiostar/Rakuten Shibuya Open RAN dataset lets us answer with measured numbers rather than architecture rhetoric — the empirical spine of this paper's falsifiable claim. This is the home of the Shibuya self-healing result, stated here in full and referenced by name thereafter.

### 4.1 The dataset

WINNIIO analyzed a production mobility dataset from the Rakuten Shibuya dense-urban cluster: 75 cells and 12,819,049 handover attempts. The raw counters are production data [deployed]; the handover-outcome classification carries a synthetic-mobility watermark (§2.5), so all outcome-derived figures below are labeled [demonstrated]. Baseline overall handover success was 97.87% — an aggregate that, while it hides local failures, is itself only a *soft* baseline: well-run dense-urban clusters commonly sit at 98.5–99.5%, so 97.87% would already draw operator questions. Six cells breached the problem threshold (handover failure >5%, ping-pong >2.7%).

A caveat on failure decomposition. The canonical mobility-robustness taxonomy — too-early, too-late, wrong-cell (Nguyen & Kwon, 2018) — is properly derived from RLF reports and re-establishment events, which this dataset does not contain. From attempt counters plus ping-pong timing alone, we classify failures heuristically: relations with rapid return handovers are scored ping-pong/too-early; relations whose failures concentrate toward a non-serving neighbor are scored wrong-cell. On that heuristic, wrong-cell handovers account for roughly 72% of failures on otherwise healthy cells — a **labeled inference from counters, not an RLF-verified split** [demonstrated, inference]. The direction is what matters: wrong-cell dominance is the signature that Cell Individual Offset (CIO), the per-neighbor spatial bias, is the correct lever, whereas too-early/too-late failures would point to timing (time-to-trigger, hysteresis).

### 4.2 The intervention, honestly measured

The self-healing action was CIO tuning through Mobility Robustness Optimization: shift the offset on a mistuned neighbor relation by 1–2 dB so the handover boundary moves off the fade cliff. Across 22 re-optimized neighbor relations, problem-cell handover success rose from approximately 84% to approximately 92% on the acted relations — a mean improvement of +8.0 percentage points, with a maximum of +11.1 points [demonstrated]. Two numbers must not be conflated: the ~84% baseline and the ~92% endpoint are the attempt-weighted mean *across the acted relations* (84 + 8.0 = 92), while +11.1 is the single best-improving relation. Earlier internal drafts quoted a ~93–94% endpoint; that figure conflated the per-relation mean with a single-cell peak and is corrected here and reconciled once in the Appendix claim ledger. This is not a simulation; it is a closed-loop optimization applied to real production counters. It converges with the independent O-RAN near-RT RIC result of Kitagawa et al. (2023), who cut handover failure rate from 1.8% to 0.4% via real-time A3-event threshold tuning over the E2SM-RC interface [demonstrated] — different network, same mechanism, same direction of travel. The mechanistic literature (Asghar et al., 2018; Onireti et al., 2016) frames this as one of the three SON pillars; our contribution is a *measured* instance on a nationwide commercial Open RAN, not a testbed.

**The zero-gaslight footnote.** Naive reporting would quote the mean success rate across relations and claim a larger, cleaner lift. When we reconciled the mean-rate against the attempt-weighted rate, unweighted averaging inflated success by roughly 0.7–0.8 points [demonstrated]. Small cells with few attempts but near-perfect rates drag the mean upward; weighting by the 12.8M actual attempts corrects it. We report the weighted number and name the inflation. This is the data-integrity discipline the whole paper argues for: an autonomous system that optimizes against a vanity metric will confidently heal the wrong thing. Reality — attempts that actually happened — is the arbiter, not the flattering average.

### 4.3 What this is worth

Handover failure is not a cosmetic KPI. A wrong-cell handover is a dropped or stalled call at the exact moment a user is moving — the experience that drives churn. Consulting and vendor sources put a value on that experience: KPMG is reported to find that 45% of smartphone users name poor network quality as their primary reason for switching providers [vendor-reported, secondary] (Tridens Technology, 2024, citing KPMG); acquiring a replacement subscriber is reported to cost 6–7× retaining one [vendor-reported] (STL Partners, 2023); and McKinsey is reported to tie a 1% churn reduction to roughly a 5% profit lift [vendor-reported, secondary] (Tridens Technology, 2024, citing McKinsey). These are cross-industry heuristics carried through a vendor blog, not telecom-specific audited unit economics, and Chapter 7 bounds how much of that churn a handover fix can actually claim. Lifting 22 problem relations off the fade cliff is therefore not a radio-engineering curiosity, but the size of the prize is a *bounded projection*, not a demonstrated figure. Related production xApps report ping-pong reductions and handover-success gains of similar shape (Alsuhli et al., 2022) [demonstrated], reinforcing that the Shibuya numbers sit inside a credible envelope rather than at an outlier edge.

### 4.4 Where this sits on the autonomy ladder

Mapped to the TM Forum Autonomous Networks framework, this loop is **Level 3 (conditional autonomy), scenario-scoped to the mobility-robustness use case**: the system detects the degraded relations, decides the CIO adjustment, and acts, but a human still sets policy and reviews outcomes (TM Forum, 2024a). Per the IG1230 framework, autonomy is assessed per task across awareness, analysis, decision, and execution within a scoped domain — not as a global grade. Human policy-setting-and-review is the defining signature of L3; **L4 would require removing the human from the review loop and demonstrating self-optimization across varying network conditions and domains without operator policy intervention.** This loop does neither, so it is L3 with an explicit L4 gap, and it is single-scenario, not network-wide.

The operator economics reported for L4 belong to specific certified use cases, and are stated as such. China Mobile reports a >30% cut in backend O&M manpower and roughly 30% lower fault/complaint MTTR in a single certified NOC use case; Telefónica Vivo reports up to 90% efficiency gains in route convergence in a single network-creation use case (TM Forum, 2024a, 2024b) [TM Forum-reported operator self-claim, single certified use case]. These are use-case-scoped, forum-reported figures, not organization-wide L4 and not independently verified — no operator has reached organizational L4. Rakuten's own nationwide RIC platform targets a 15–20% energy reduction via predictive rApps (Rakuten Mobile & Rakuten Symphony, 2025a) [target], with a trial demonstrating up to 25% on the RIC (Rakuten Mobile & Rakuten Symphony, 2025b) [demonstrated]. The self-healing MRO in this chapter is the *quality* analogue of that *energy* work: the same closed loop, the same L3 rung, a different objective.

In Grieves' digital-twin terms, the Shibuya twin does all three functions on live counters: it **replicates** the handover state of 75 cells, **predicts** which neighbor relations will fail from the too-early/too-late/wrong-cell signature, and **navigates** the network back toward health by re-issuing CIO (Grieves, 2019). The twin is the boundary object — the shared, machine-actionable representation of reality that the RIC, the NOC engineer, and the optimization loop all read from and write to.

### 4.5 The honest boundary of "quantum"

Where does quantum enter *this* case? Narrowly, and not naturally. CIO tuning is natively a *continuous, small-dimensional* problem — real-valued 1–2 dB offsets on a graph — so casting it as a QUBO forces a discretization and constraint-encoding that the problem does not ask for. On the 75-cell Shibuya graph the classical continuous solver wins decisively on latency, cost, and interpretability, partly *because* QUBO-ification is an artificial constraint here, and we say so plainly. The genuinely combinatorial companions are where the quantum-inspired (QI) framing earns its place: alarm/neighbor correlation and cell-outage-compensation selection are QUBO-shaped matching problems at fleet scale. These are QI *candidates*, benchmark-gated against any future quantum solver under the author-proposed DTC maturity progression (QA→QI→QR→HQC→QN), which admits a quantum path only when it clears a measured benchmark. The quantum relevance is a *solver-interchange* argument, not a performance claim: the optimization is written to a schema that lets the solver be swapped without rewriting the objective, so the day a quantum-relevant advantage clears the gate, the loop inherits it. Until that day, the seed of quantum here is architectural readiness, not runtime. That restraint is the point: the falsifiable thesis of this paper does not depend on quantum at all — it depends on whether reality stays readable and the loop stays sovereign when the network is under stress, which the next chapter puts to the disaster test.

## 5. Why Today Cannot Carry Tomorrow: The Excel City, Audited

Before any city earns the adjective "quantum," it owes itself an audit of the instruments that actually run it today — and the audit does not flatter. Beneath the procurement decks and the intent dashboards, four secondary instruments quietly stand in for reality: the spreadsheet, the regression, the vendor cloud, and the paper trust. Each is mistaken for the primary construct it is meant to approximate. This chapter names each substitution, prices it, and pairs it with the SMILE correction it violates — then grounds two of the four in the primary deployment data of Case A and Case B, and calls established disaster and case figures back by name rather than re-quoting them.

### 5.1 The spreadsheet mistaken for the plant

Municipal energy planning, maintenance budgeting, and capacity forecasting still run, operationally, on cells. The field evidence on that substrate is settled: roughly 86–94% of operational spreadsheets carry at least one error, large models almost certainly contain an incorrect bottom-line value, the errors resist detection, and developers stay systematically overconfident about all of it [demonstrated] (Panko, 2016). Gartner's reference customers put the average annual cost of poor data quality at no less than $12.9 million per organization [demonstrated] (Gartner, 2021). Crucially, these are not the costs of *missing* data — they are the costs of *data-first* practice: collect first, govern later, discover the defect at decision time by watching the decision fail (NetSuite, 2024). SMILE's NUDEDA lens inverts the pyramid — Outcome → Action → Insight → Information → Data — so every datum carries a traceable justification back to the need it serves, which is the only ordering under which a quality defect is discoverable *before* it becomes an operational one (Waern, 2026).

### 5.2 The regression mistaken for the future

The Excel city's second substitution is methodological, and it survives the migration to machine learning intact: fit a curve to yesterday, extrapolate to tomorrow. The theory is unambiguous about when this breaks — out-of-sample performance collapses under structural breaks and parameter nonconstancy [peer-reviewed-theory] (Xu & Perron, 2019), precisely the regime a city under densification, retrofit, and climate shift permanently inhabits. A regression trained on a building's pre-retrofit heating seasons is not a cautious model of its post-retrofit self; it is a confident model of a building that no longer exists.

Case A shows the correction as built artifact, not slogan. The twin does not regress the dead past; it runs AEST's *Emulate* operation on the living present, front-running the reference load established in §3 so the room neither overshoots nor drops [deployed]. This is the difference between statistics and simulation — the regression asks what yesterday implies; the emulated twin asks what the plant, as it is right now, will do next. Honesty about the case sharpens rather than softens the point: as §3 records, the preschool showed no measurable gain and two schools fought commissioning-stage regulation problems [deployed]. The twin's value there was not yet thermal — it was that technicians, users, and vendors could all *see* the same reality and locate the fault, the boundary-object role WINNIIO added beyond the tender (Waern, 2026).

### 5.3 The average mistaken for the truth

Case B exposes a subtler substitution: the summary statistic that flatters. As §4.2 establishes, naive mean-rate reporting overstated Shibuya handover success by roughly 0.7–0.8 percentage points against the attempt-weighted rate — small, but the exact direction that turns a struggling network into a green dashboard [demonstrated]. Weighting by attempts corrects it, and only the corrected figure earns a claim label. On that honest baseline, the Shibuya self-healing result (§4) did real work. The lesson generalizes past radio: a metric that averages away the attempts it is built from is the same epistemic error as the spreadsheet — a secondary instrument reporting on itself rather than on reality. Zero-gaslight metric integrity is not a virtue add-on; it is the precondition for trusting any autonomy claim built on top.

### 5.4 The cloud mistaken for the ground — and the harvest it invites

The third substitution is topological: the default architecture ships data *away* — to a vendor region, an analytics platform, a foreign jurisdiction — to be understood far from where it was born. Concentration converts a single fault into a civic one; the Iberian partition (§1.1) is the continental-scale proof, and Case A lived its miniature when the Växjö *strömavbrott* mid-deployment became the project's unplanned degraded-mode test — the moment that separates a twin which stays readable locally from one whose intelligence evaporates when the link drops [deployed].

State ONCE, here, the permanence trap: centralization also converts today's recording into tomorrow's breach. Adversaries already harvest encrypted traffic to decrypt once a cryptographically relevant quantum computer exists — *harvest-now, decrypt-later* — and the risk scales with the data's required confidentiality lifetime [demonstrated] (Keyfactor, n.d.), a threat now modelled as a live systemic exposure by a G7 monetary authority, not a hypothetical [peer-reviewed-theory] (Bordo & Federal Reserve Board, 2025). A perpetual recording of a city's occupancy and utility telemetry is exactly that long-horizon asset. The architectural answer is not to record less but to record *locally*: raw data stays at the edge where it was born — the owner-chooses design Vöfab implements, self-learning in-building or optional third-party cloud, never mandatory egress [deployed] — while only formulated problems and privacy-protected aggregates cross the boundary, encrypted under the now-ratified NIST primitives FIPS 203, 204, and 205 [deployed] (NIST, 2024). What never leaves cannot be harvested in transit.

### 5.5 The paper trust mistaken for legitimacy

The final substitution is institutional, and Toronto remains its canonical exhibit: Sidewalk Labs' Quayside proposed an Urban Data Trust that could not say what problem it solved, to whom it answered, or how it related to existing law, and had shed its Privacy-by-Design advisers before cancellation in 2020 [demonstrated] (Goodman & Powles, 2019). Read through Actor-Network Theory, the diagnosis is exact: a governance body not enrolled as an obligatory passage point by the communities it claims to govern collects legitimacy the way an unenrolled sensor collects data — in answer to questions nobody posed (Waern, 2026). The corrective is to define the boundary box before thinking outside it: make reality itself the boundary-spanning object residents, regulators, and operators read differently but share physically, and gate every AI output through retrieval-over-generation provenance rather than asking citizens to trust an abstraction.

Each of the five failures has the same shape — a secondary instrument mistaken for the primary construct. The chapters that follow build in the opposite order, from reality outward, and call these figures back by name rather than re-quoting them.

## 6. Reality Under Duress: Wildfires, Hurricanes, Earthquakes

A network operator earns the title "self-healing" only against an adversary that does not schedule itself. Chapter 4's Shibuya case tuned handovers on a network that stayed up; the disaster is the test the vendor slide omits, because the disaster removes the two things every architecture in this paper quietly assumes — power and backhaul. This chapter runs four recent events as the falsification trials for the central thesis. If reality can only be read while the cloud answers, then reality was never the primary construct; it was a lease on someone else's uptime.

### 6.1 What IS: the network and its data die together

Start with the canonical grid-collapse artifact, the Iberian partition established at §1.1 (ENTSO-E, 2025 [demonstrated]). Its lesson is not that comms broke. It is that comms and the reality they were meant to observe went dark in the same instant, from a single upstream cause: telecommunications, rail signalling, traffic control, and emergency dispatch failed *with* the grid, not after it.

Hurricanes make the backhaul half of the failure explicit — the Helene backhaul severance established at §1.1 (Congressional Research Service, 2024; FCC, 2024; Fierce Network, 2024; Asheville Watchdog, 2025 [demonstrated]). The binding constraint was not the radio: even where power and radios were fine, severed transport was the limiting factor, leaving sites like Chimney Rock offline after power returned. A radio with nothing to talk to is a partition, and a partition is precisely the state a cloud-dependent twin cannot survive.

Then the data itself. Helene disabled NOAA's National Centers for Environmental Information in Asheville — power, internet, and cooling-water loss forced improvised cooling from fire-department tankers to protect the national climate archive (NC State University News, 2025) [demonstrated]. The near-loss of a designated archive during the very event it should have recorded is the OAIS nightmare (CCSDS, 2012) made physical: reality writing to a store that the disaster can reach.

Earthquakes remove even the reporting. On 24 June 2026 two strike-slip quakes (reported ~M7.2 then ~M7.5, roughly 39 seconds apart) near Veroes, Yaracuy caused provisional casualty counts of more than 3,500 killed and over 16,400 injured; water, electricity, gas, and communications failed together, and the sparse figures were attributed partly to a communications and media blackout (magnitudes: USGS, 2026 [demonstrated]; provisional casualties: U.S. Department of State, 2026; 2026 Venezuela earthquakes, n.d. [demonstrated, provisional/secondary]). Fresh-event casualty figures are volatile and are carried here as provisional, not verified. When the network dies, reality does not merely go unregulated — it goes unread and unremembered.

### 6.2 The through-line to Case A

None of this is exotic to the operator, because Case A already ran the experiment at building scale. The WINNIIO "Smart Värme" deployment in Växjö lost mains power mid-project, and the edge-native controllers kept regulating the schools locally on their own recorded model of thermal reality [deployed]. That outage was not an interruption of the case study; it *was* the case study — a micro-instance of the identical failure class the Iberian partition, the Helene backhaul severance, and the Yaracuy blackout express at civilizational scale. The difference between six sensors in a Swedish classroom and 75 cells in Shibuya is resolution, not kind. Both face the same question: when the link to the centre drops, does the local node still hold a readable, actuatable model of its own physical reality — or was it only ever a terminal for a decision made elsewhere?

### 6.3 What WILL BE: the degraded-mode sovereign edge

The answer is being rehearsed in the field, and the rehearsals are operator-led rather than aspirational. During Hurricane Melissa (winds >165 mph) in late October 2025, Liberty Caribbean activated Starlink Direct-to-Cell as satellite backhaul so that compatible LTE phones on Flow's network gained a satellite link with no special equipment when towers went offline, with capacity prioritised to western Jamaica (Data Center Dynamics, 2025; Liberty Latin America, 2025) [deployed]. (A widely-circulated "~600 units" figure could not be confirmed against the primary Liberty/Data Center Dynamics releases and is omitted as an unverified press count.) During Helene, the FCC granted SpaceX/T-Mobile emergency authority to broadcast Wireless Emergency Alerts to phones on all networks in North Carolina (RCR Wireless News, 2024) [deployed], and AT&T's FirstNet Response Operations Group ran its largest response in history — 220+ public-safety restorations using Compact Rapid Deployables that link via satellite and, critically, do not rely on commercial power (AT&T, 2024) [deployed]. Across hurricanes, floods, and wildfires, Starlink reports more than 1.5 million people relying on Direct-to-Cell when terrestrial networks were down [demonstrated in-field, vendor spec] (SpaceX, 2025).

The contrast with Hurricane Beryl sharpens the design point. After Beryl struck Jamaica on ~3 July 2024, Digicel had restored 96% of mobile and 98% of fixed-line customers by 29 July — yet the primary blocker to full restoration was not damage but the absence of commercial power keeping sites off-air, worsened when diesel was stolen from the generators that were meant to bridge the gap (Jamaica Information Service, 2024) [demonstrated]. Sixteen months later, on the same island, satellite failover turned "wait for the grid" into "reroute to orbit." That is the degraded-mode sovereign edge maturing between two storms: rehearsed failover from a twin that knew, in advance, which cells would island and which backhaul path to fall to.

Southern Europe supplies the wildfire face of the same requirement. The 2023 Alexandroupolis fire — ~73,000 ha, the EU's largest recorded single wildfire at the time — caused a power outage on Samothraki (2023 Greece wildfires, n.d.) [demonstrated, secondary], and a Greek fire-brigade report attributed nine major 2024 fires to the electricity network itself (2024 Greek wildfires, n.d.) [demonstrated, secondary]. Grid infrastructure as both victim and ignition source is the strongest possible argument for actuation that survives its own upstream: the network node most likely to start or feel the fire is the one that must keep regulating without the grid.

### 6.4 The falsifier, restated as a design requirement

The archival half closes the loop. Reality recorded locally and signed for permanence — FAIR, self-describing, OAIS-preserved (Wilkinson et al., 2016; CCSDS, 2012), and encrypted under the ratified PQC primitives FIPS 203/204/205 (NIST, 2024) so that a harvested recording survives a future decryptor (Bordo & Federal Reserve Board, 2025) — is reality that stays readable *irrespective of technology and irrespective of the cloud's reachability*. What never leaves the edge cannot be trapped when the backhaul is cut, and cannot be lost when the archive floods.

This yields the paper's single falsifiable requirement, restated here as design language: **if the twin cannot run partitioned, it was never sovereign.** The partition test is not an edge case to handle after launch; it is the experiment that decides whether the architecture is what it claims to be. Case A illustrated it under a real outage. Melissa, Helene, and Beryl show operators rehearsing it on purpose. The Iberian partition, the Yaracuy blackout, and the Asheville archive show what failing it costs. Every subsequent decision in this paper — solver-interchange, PQC-at-archive, MIM conformance — is downstream of this one gate: reality must remain readable, actuatable, and recorded at the edge when the centre is gone, or the word "sovereign" is decoration.

## 7. The Operator Ledger: ARPU, Churn, and the Price of Healing

A network operator does not buy self-healing because it is elegant. She buys it because her top line has stopped growing and her cost line has not. GSMA Intelligence puts global mobile-service revenue on a roughly 1.8% CAGR through 2030, with average revenue per user (ARPU) flat-to-declining as subscriber growth saturates [deployed] (GSMA Intelligence, 2025). Against that flat ceiling sits an energy floor that keeps rising: the radio access network is reported to consume roughly 73% of a mobile operator's total network energy, and a 5G base station can draw up to 70% more power than its 4G equivalent [peer-reviewed-theory] (Mughees et al., 2021) — both figures are configuration-dependent ceilings, not universal constants, and are used as such below. When revenue is fixed and the largest controllable cost is energy, every watt and every retained subscriber becomes a margin decision. This chapter prices the Shibuya self-healing result (§4) — where CIO tuning lifted problem-cell handover success from ~84% to ~92% on the acted relations, mean +8.0 points, peak +11.1 [demonstrated] — against those two levers, and states where the operator earns genuinely *new* ARPU rather than merely defending the old.

### 7.1 The churn mechanism: a dropped handover is a resignation letter

The economic bridge from a handover statistic to a P&L line runs through churn, but the bridge carries several toll-gates the naive version skips. The reported quality-churn figures are cross-industry, vendor-carried heuristics: 45% of smartphone users are reported to name poor network quality as their primary switching reason [vendor-reported, secondary] (Tridens Technology, 2024, citing KPMG); acquiring a new customer is reported to cost six to seven times retaining one [vendor-reported] (STL Partners, 2023); a one-point churn reduction is reported to lift profit ~5% [vendor-reported, secondary] (Tridens Technology, 2024, citing McKinsey). Critically, a failed handover is only *one* of several drivers of perceived "poor network quality" — coverage holes, indoor penetration, data speed, and congestion typically dominate. So the self-healing loop removes a *fraction* of the specific events behind the 45%, not the whole of quality-driven churn; §7.2 bounds that fraction rather than banking it.

### 7.2 A disciplined valuation of eight points

Consider what the +8-point improvement is worth, stated as a model with every assumption exposed and every figure labeled illustrative. Take a mid-sized carrier of 10 million subscribers at a €20 blended monthly ARPU (a European-to-Asian range; a US-postpaid $55 base would scale this 2–3× upward). Gross service revenue is €2.4 billion per year, and each full percentage point of annual churn therefore places €24 million of *gross* revenue at risk.

The load-bearing discipline is refusing to bank that gross figure and refusing to double-count. Handover failures are one contributor among several to network-quality churn, so the attributable share is small — a defensible band is low single-digit percent of quality-driven churn, which itself is under half of total churn. If the CIO-tuning loop shaves even 10–30 basis points off annual churn under that bounding, the retained gross revenue is €2.4–7.2 million. Valuing that retained revenue requires picking *one* basis, not stacking two on the same euros: either value the avoided *replacements* at the six-to-seven-times acquisition-cost differential (STL Partners, 2023), **or** value the retained *revenue* at an EBITDA margin of 35–45% — not both, since that would count the same margin dollar twice. On the retained-margin basis alone, the recurring benefit lands in the low-to-mid seven figures annually for this single carrier [projection, derived from vendor-reported industry heuristics, not from primary operator P&L, uncertainty band wide]. The point is not the euro figure, which any operator can recompute on its own base and its own audited quality-churn attribution; the point is the mechanism — fewer drops, fewer churn triggers — anchored to a *measured* +8-point KPI rather than a vendor promise.

### 7.3 The other lever: healing as opex, not just revenue defense

The self-healing network also cuts cost directly, and here the operator evidence — while still forum-reported and use-case-scoped — is firmer than the churn projection. China Mobile, in a single certified NOC use case at Autonomous Networks Level 4, reports a >30% reduction in backend O&M manpower and a ~30% average cut in fault/complaint MTTR [TM Forum-reported operator self-claim] (TM Forum, 2024). Telefónica Vivo, in a single network-planning use case at L4, reports up to 90% route-convergence efficiency and a 60% cut in budget-approval time [TM Forum-reported operator self-claim] (TM Forum, 2024). On energy specifically, Rakuten's nationwide RIC targets 15–20% network-energy reduction, with a trial demonstrating up to 25% (Rakuten Mobile & Rakuten Symphony, 2025) [target / demonstrated peak]; Vodafone UK and Ericsson reached up to 33% daily-power reduction at select London 5G sites through orchestrated deep-sleep [demonstrated] (Ericsson, 2025).

The opex bridge must be stated explicitly, not asserted. A RAN-energy saving translates to total-opex movement only through the chain: *RAN-energy cut × RAN share of network energy (~73%) × network energy as a share of total opex*. Energy is typically only ~5–10% of telco opex, so a 20% RAN-energy cut ≈ 0.20 × 0.73 × ~0.075 ≈ ~1% of total opex — a whole percentage point, but a *derived* one that holds only because those three terms multiply out that way, not a free-standing claim. Earned inside the existing P&L, requiring no new customer, and honest about which term carries it.

### 7.4 Twin-mediated new ARPU: selling the verified, not the pipe

Cost defense keeps the operator solvent; new revenue requires the twin to become a saleable object in its own right. Three lines are visible. First, verifiable-SLA slices: what makes a network slice sellable to an enterprise is not bandwidth but a *provable* quality guarantee — an intent monitored by the twin, with the provenance-signed audit trail as the product rather than the megabits. Second, integrated sensing-as-a-service: the same densified radio infrastructure that carries traffic doubles as a perceptive sensing grid, turning the operator's densest asset into inventory it can lease. Third, enterprise twins: the operator that already runs a live RAN twin for its own self-healing can offer campus and factory operators a managed twin of their private network. Each line monetizes an asset the self-healing architecture already forced the operator to build — the twin is amortized twice.

### 7.5 The asymptote: 100% handovers as a rehearsed contract, not a slogan

Where does the +8-point gain terminate? Not at a marketing "100%." Well-run networks already sit at 97–99% handover success, and residual failures are dominated by radio physics — deep fades, interference bursts — and user-equipment behavior that no amount of pre-computation removes. The honest [target] is therefore zero mobility-*caused* perceptible failures, approached asymptotically as the twin evolves from tuning static CIO/time-to-trigger offsets toward multi-agentic, continuously rehearsed handover policies — the O-RAN near-RT RIC path that has already driven experimental handover-failure rates from 1.8% to 0.4% [demonstrated] (Kitagawa et al., 2023), with production ping-pong reductions reported alongside. The subscriber never learns the failing radio unit was drained and its neighbors reloaded. That invisibility is the entire commercial difficulty: the absence of a dropped call cannot be shown on a bill. The operator ledger closes on this paradox — the price of healing is real, countable, and paid up front, while its return arrives as an event that, by design, never happens.

## 8. The Seed of Quantum: Protect Now, Optimize Where It Pays

The title of this paper makes a promise that most "quantum city" literature breaks: that quantum technology does real work here, now, and not merely in a keynote. This chapter keeps the promise by shrinking it. Today quantum is not a processor humming in a municipal basement; it is a *seed* — two narrow, defensible workstreams planted in architecture decisions being locked in this decade. The author-proposed DTC quantum-maturity spine (§1.4) names the whole trajectory — QA, QI, QR, HQC, QN — but only its first three rungs bear weight in 2026. This chapter defends exactly two: **QR as archival ethics** (deployable now) and **QI as bounded optimization** (deployable now, behind a gate). Everything past QR remains labeled [projection] or [target], per the discipline this paper set out to keep.

### 8.1 Protect now: post-quantum cryptography as the ethics of perpetual recording

The unit of contribution defended throughout this paper — reality as the primary, perpetually-recorded, boundary-spanning construct — carries a cryptographic consequence that ordinary security policy misses. A city, an operator, or a hospital that records reality continuously manufactures the longest-lived ciphertext in existence, and *harvest-now-decrypt-later* (HNDL) targets exactly that asset class: adversaries, including state-level signals actors, intercept and warehouse encrypted data today to decrypt once a cryptographically relevant quantum computer exists, with exposure scaling to the data's required confidentiality lifetime [demonstrated] (Keyfactor, n.d.). This is no longer a cryptographer's hypothetical. A G7 monetary authority now models HNDL as a live systemic risk to the financial system and quantifies migration urgency accordingly [peer-reviewed-theory] (Bordo & Federal Reserve Board, 2025).

The arithmetic is unforgiving: a fifty-year confidentiality horizon minus a multi-year migration cycle closes *before* the threat hardware arrives. What is new in this paper's framing is the ethical claim: **you cannot perpetually record reality on cryptography with a ten-year life.** Phase 6 of SMILE — Perpetual Wisdom, knowledge that outlives its origin project (Waern, 2026) — is therefore uncomputable without post-quantum protection at the *archive layer first*, because the archive is not the most-attacked surface but the longest-lived one.

The primitives to honor this obligation now exist. NIST ratified FIPS 203 (ML-KEM, key encapsulation), FIPS 204 (ML-DSA, lattice signatures), and FIPS 205 (SLH-DSA, stateless hash-based signatures) on 13 August 2024, effective immediately [deployed] (NIST, 2024), with the issuance recorded in the Federal Register as ratified federal standards, not drafts (U.S. Department of Commerce, 2024). ML-KEM replaces RSA/ECDH key exchange; ML-DSA and SLH-DSA replace classical signatures (Cloud Security Alliance, 2024) [roadmap]. Deployment, honestly, lags the standards — production TLS and PKI rollout remains early [roadmap] (DigiCert, 2025) — which is precisely why "seed," not "harvest," is the correct verb. The migration deadline that justifies acting today is authoritative: CNSA 2.0 requires new national-security acquisitions to be PQC-capable by January 2027 and full quantum resistance by 2035 [target] (National Security Agency, 2022). The transition safeguard is crypto-agility: the capability to swap cryptographic algorithms across protocols and hardware *without interrupting a running system*, with hybrid classical-plus-PQC as the bridge [roadmap] (NIST, 2025).

Crucially, PQC-at-archive is not only a defensive control; it is what makes reality *available to others irrespective of technology and time* — the same durability logic that OAIS (ISO 14721) encodes for preserving digital objects across generations of technology and designated communities [deployed] (CCSDS, 2012), that FAIR encodes for machine-actionable, self-describing data usable irrespective of originating technology [peer-reviewed-theory] (Wilkinson et al., 2016), and that the EU Data Act now makes legal obligation through mandated interoperability, provider-switching, and safeguards against third-country access to EU data [deployed] (European Commission, 2025). Sovereign-yet-shareable is not a slogan; it is a stack — Gaia-X's federated trust framework [demonstrated] (Gaia-X, 2022) and IDS connectors with usage-control policies [demonstrated] (IDSA, n.d.) — and PQC is the layer that lets that stack outlive the vendor that built it.

### 8.2 Optimize where it pays: QI behind the benchmark gate

The second seed is optimization, and here the discipline is subtraction. Most urban and network problems worth solving reduce to quadratic assignment — QUBO-shaped combinatorics. Two survive the honesty test today, both at the QI rung. First, the RAN self-healing case: as §4.5 states, CIO tuning is natively continuous and ran and won *classically* [deployed] — it is a QI *candidate* only for its genuinely combinatorial companions (alarm/neighbor correlation, cell-outage-compensation selection), not a QI deployment, and this paper will not launder it into one. Second, building and portfolio scheduling — changeover and multi-line sequencing — runs today on CMOS annealers benchmarked competitive with specialized classical solvers on dense instances [demonstrated] (Aramon et al., 2019), with production-planning trials already deployed at industrial scale [deployed] (Fujitsu Limited & Toyota Systems Corporation, 2022).

The gate that keeps QI honest is singular: **a quantum or quantum-inspired solver replaces the classical baseline only on the day it wins on wall-clock and cost, on the same problem instance — and not one day earlier.** HQC and QN entry points (portfolio-scale joint optimization, quantum-chemistry dosing) remain [projection] precisely because they have not passed this gate.

### 8.3 The artifact that earns the billing: the Solver-Neutral Problem-Interchange Schema

A title is earned by a deliverable, not an adjective. This paper names one: a **solver-neutral problem-interchange schema with mandatory benchmark disclosure (SNPI/BD)** — proposed as a **TM Forum Open-API / information-model artifact** (with optional O-RAN rApp/xApp interface alignment and optional OASC data-model alignment), *not* an OASC Minimal Interoperability Mechanism, since a QUBO/Ising solver-interchange schema governs optimization-problem exchange rather than smart-city context/data-model interoperability. Its function is to express any optimization instance (CIO tuning, alarm correlation, scheduling) in a solver-agnostic QUBO/Ising form so the twin's data model *does not change* when the solver underneath swaps from CMOS annealer to classical heuristic to QPU — the crypto-agility principle (NIST, 2025) [roadmap] applied to solvers rather than ciphers. Its second, inseparable half is a benchmark-disclosure record: every optimization claim must ship its baseline, hardware, problem size, and wall-clock, labeled [deployed]/[demonstrated]/[projection]. SNPI/BD converts "quantum-ready" from a procurement adjective into a testable interface — vendor-independent over time, falsifiable on contact. That is what earns the seed its place in the title: not a quantum computer the city does not have, but the schema that guarantees the city can adopt one the moment — and only the moment — it wins.

## 9. Interoperability Is the Constitution: MIMs and the Boundary Object

Chapter 8 established the constitutional analogy — minimal mechanisms as sparse, binding clauses. This chapter descends from the analogy to the mechanism: what, precisely, two systems exchange at each MIM, and what breaks when they do not. The Open & Agile Smart Cities profile fixes seven load-bearing clauses — MIM1 Context, MIM2 Data Models, MIM3 Contracts, MIM4 Trust and Personal Data, MIM5 Transparency, MIM6 Security, MIM7 Places (Open & Agile Smart Cities & Living-in.EU Technical Sub-Group, 2024) [deployed]. Reading them at mechanism level exposes where the Case A and Case B deployments actually interoperate, and where a disaster severs them.

### 9.1 MIM1 as the boundary box, MIM2 as the shared grammar

MIM1 does one thing: it names *whose* reality is under discussion, at what spatial and temporal extent. Actor-Network Theory supplies its ontology — sensors, occupants, and institutions enter the record as peer actors, not as a hierarchy of subject and instrument (Waern, 2026). The practical artifact is a boundary box with three declared fields: the anchor entity, the spatial extent (a MIM7 geospatial referent), and the temporal window. A context broker that cannot populate those three fields has not defined its box, and every downstream clause inherits the vagueness.

Consider the first concrete interoperation, drawn from Case A. The *building* twin exposes each room as a MIM1 context entity — anchor: the room; extent: its cadastral coordinate; window: live plus a perpetual trace. MIM2 supplies the shared grammar: the room's heat-demand attribute is expressed in an NGSI-LD data model whose semantics the *municipal district-heating* twin already understands, so the two twins interoperate without sharing a codebase — the demonstrated federated pattern of NGSI-LD brokers under a Gaia-X trust frame integrating energy and lighting across a live platform (Cirillo et al., 2024) [demonstrated]. The building need not adopt the utility's stack; it need only speak MIM1+MIM2 at the seam. This is the entire minimal-mechanism wager, made physical in one school.

The falsifier arrives on its own: during the project, the Case A power outage partitioned the school from every upstream system [deployed]. Under a cloud-first design the exchange dies with the link. Under the edge-native design the boundary box persists locally: the building twin keeps regulating on its last-known MIM2 model and accrues its reality trace, then re-synchronises the municipal twin on reconnection. The MIM seam is not merely a data format; it is the reattachment point after a partition — the property the Iberian partition (§1.1) showed no centralised design possesses.

### 9.2 MIM3/4/5 across communities that share no ontology

The building case exercises context and data models between two municipal actors. The harder interoperation crosses communities with no common vocabulary — and this is where MIM3 Contracts, MIM4 Trust, and MIM5 Transparency do the real work.

Take the second interoperation: a network operator's twin (the Shibuya self-healing result, §4) and an emergency-services twin during a disaster. When a wildfire or hurricane degrades the network, the operator twin must hand a slice of its live reality to responders who share none of its RAN ontology. MIM3 encodes the *contract*: which slice, for which purpose, with which usage restriction. MIM4 encodes *trust*: a verifiable credential proving the responder is who they claim before any slice is released. MIM5 encodes *transparency*: which automated action — a cell-outage-compensation retune, a CIO adjustment — the operator's system took, rendered explainable to a non-RAN reader.

These are not hypothetical clauses. The U.S. FCC activated the Mandatory Disaster Response Initiative during the Helene backhaul severance (§1.1), compelling cross-network roaming while sites sat offline (Congressional Research Service, 2024; Federal Communications Commission, 2024) [demonstrated]. Mandatory roaming *is* a MIM3-plus-MIM4 act — an inter-operator data-and-service *contract* (and its identity/trust exchange) imposed by regulation precisely because voluntary interoperation failed under load. When terrestrial mechanisms collapsed entirely, operators fell back to a rehearsed partition mitigation: Liberty Caribbean activated Starlink Direct-to-Cell after Hurricane Melissa so standard LTE phones gained a satellite link with no new equipment (Data Center Dynamics, 2025; Liberty Latin America, 2025) [deployed]. The failover is a MIM-shaped act — a pre-negotiated trust-and-contract path that lets an operator twin keep exposing reality to responders when its own towers go silent.

Across both interoperations, one object spans the seam: not a shared model, but reality itself, digitally rendered — the boundary-spanning object precisely because the building, the utility, the operator, and the emergency service share no ontology yet share the same physical scene (Waern, 2026). Two distinct mechanisms carry the seam and must not be conflated: the intent interface (TMF921) expresses the declarative *what* — an autonomous domain's intent to restore or compensate service — while the *inter-operator sharing itself* is a business agreement (MIM3 Contracts, e.g. TMF-order/agreement APIs) plus identity and trust (MIM4). Mandatory roaming is the contract-and-trust analogue; TMF921 is the intent-negotiation analogue. They are different layers of the same act.

### 9.3 Governing a perpetual record

A perpetual reality record turns three MIMs into governance of constitutional grade. *Who may query which slice, at which zoom, over which window* is a MIM4 question the boundary box must answer per query, because a decade-deep occupancy trace queried at room resolution is a surveillance act regardless of intent. *Explainability as a right* is MIM5 made non-negotiable: no automated decision drawn from the record ships without provenance a domestic stakeholder can read. And archival ethics is the clause ordinary security misses. Adversaries harvest ciphertext now to decrypt when quantum arrives — a live economic risk a G7 monetary authority now models, not a hypothetical (Bordo & Federal Reserve Board, 2025; Keyfactor, n.d.) [peer-reviewed-theory]. A record built to outlive its assets must therefore adopt the finalised NIST post-quantum standards — FIPS 203, 204, 205 (National Institute of Standards and Technology, 2024) [deployed] — at the archive layer first, inside an OAIS-style preservation model designed to keep digital objects understandable across generations of technology and designated communities (Consultative Committee for Space Data Systems, 2012) [deployed], so reality stays readable irrespective of the vendor or format that recorded it — the FAIR principle stated for infrastructure (Wilkinson et al., 2016) [peer-reviewed-theory].

### 9.4 Where the clauses land

The governance regimes describe the same seam under different names. Mapping them keeps the constitution from fragmenting into rival standards:

| MIM (OASC) | TM Forum ODA function | ITU-T Y.4489 federation role |
|---|---|---|
| MIM1 Context / MIM7 Places | Party & context management; Intent (TMF921) as machine-actionable context | Twin registry + shared spatial reference |
| MIM2 Data Models | Information framework (SID) | Common data model for federated twins |
| MIM3 Contracts | Business agreement / product order (incl. intent-derived service contracts) | Inter-twin service and access agreement |
| MIM4 Trust & Personal Data | Identity & access management | Credential and authorisation exchange |
| MIM5 Transparency | AI governance & decision-logging (explainability) | Explainable cross-twin decision record |

Note the two corrections this table encodes: TMF921 (Intent Management) belongs against MIM1/MIM3 as machine-actionable context and contract, not against MIM5; and MIM5 Transparency maps to AI-governance and decision-logging, which is an AI-management function, not an intent-API function. ITU-T Y.4489 standardises exactly this federation of independent twins through registries and communication interfaces (ITU-T, 2023) [roadmap]. Read together, the three frameworks agree on the same minimal seam — which is the strongest available evidence that the seam, not any single stack, is what a city is actually signing.

## 10. Conclusion: Five Decisions That Reverb

### 10.1 Consolidated Limitations

This paper is an architecture-position synthesis anchored by two primary cases, not a controlled multi-site study, and its limitations are gathered here rather than left scattered. First, single-author provenance: both primary cases were conducted by the author, and no independent party has re-analyzed the raw telemetry; the SMILE lens is simultaneously the paper's method and an artifact under evaluation, a circularity declared in §1.4 and not resolved here. Second, Case A (Vöfab/Växjö) is one municipality, three buildings, with a partition event that was illustrative and accidental rather than a controlled failover trial; §3 states what a rigorous partition test would require, and that test remains future work. Third, Case B (Shibuya) is one dense-urban cluster; its 97.87% aggregate baseline sits below the 98.5–99.5% dense-urban norm and is treated as a soft baseline, and the 500-UE mobility traces used for RF-twin illustration are explicitly synthetic. Fourth, several disaster figures (notably the Venezuela twin-quake sequence and some Southern-Europe wildfire counts) are carried as [demonstrated, provisional/secondary] on secondary reporting and should be re-verified before any operational use. Fifth, all economic figures are illustrative models built on labeled industry estimates, with the margin-versus-gross-revenue distinction made explicit but the absolute magnitudes dependent on assumptions a given operator must substitute. Sixth, the quantum contribution is deliberately narrow: PQC is deployable now, but the quantum-optimization claims are QI-stage and benchmark-gated, and no fault-tolerant result is claimed. The claim ledger (Appendix) maps every headline number to its tier so a reader can audit the gap between what is shown and what is argued.

Every chapter of this paper converges on the unit of contribution stated in full at §1.3: reality as the primary, perpetually-recorded, boundary-spanning construct across operator, city, and clinic, and the sovereign, explainable, quantum-safe architecture decisions being locked in now. It is not a forecast; it is a fork. Five decisions, taken in this decade's procurements, determine which of the three cities a network operator inhabits in 2076. Each decision has an evidence file and a fifty-year compounding cost.

**Decision one — edge-native versus cloud-first.** The falsifier of this entire thesis is the partition test: when the network dies, does reality remain readable and actionable *locally*? Case A illustrated it under load — during the Vöfab outage the edge-native controllers held local state and resumed without a cloud round-trip, because insight lived where reality happened [deployed]. The what-IS contrast is documented at continental scale by the Iberian partition and the Helene backhaul severance (§1.1) [demonstrated]. A cloud-first city compounds this into a half-century liability: every year of centralized dependence is another year in which a single region, update, or backhaul cut renders reality unreadable. The self-healing answer is not heroism but rehearsal — AT&T's FirstNet CRDs and Liberty Caribbean's Starlink Direct-to-Cell failover during Hurricane Melissa were pre-arranged degraded-mode paths, not improvisation [deployed] (AT&T, 2024; Liberty Latin America, 2025).

**Decision two — need-first versus data-first.** NUDEDA is cheap to adopt now and ruinous to retrofit. Case B is the proof: Altiostar/Rakuten's Shibuya Open RAN did not accumulate data and hope. It proceeded from a defined need — lift problem-cell handover success — and CIO tuning across 75 cells and 12.8M attempts raised success on the acted relations from ~84% to ~92% (mean +8.0 pts, peak +11.1) [demonstrated]. The compounding cost of the inverse is the Excel city audited in Chapter 5: fifty years of ungoverned collection whose defects surface only when a decision fails.

**Decision three — minimal-interop versus lock-in.** A record that cannot be read across vendors or across time is not sovereign; it is a hostage. MIM1 (context) and MIM7 (marketplace/portability) conformance, FAIR metadata, and OAIS archival modelling make reality readable irrespective of the originating technology [demonstrated] (Wilkinson et al., 2016; CCSDS/ISO 14721, 2012). The EU Data Act now makes this law, not preference, banning egress fees by January 2027 [deployed] (European Commission, 2025). Skip it, and the 2026 ontology becomes the toll booth every 2076 query pays.

**Decision four — PQC-now versus later.** Perpetual recording is a perpetual liability the moment it is captured, because harvest-now-decrypt-later treats any long-confidentiality asset as already exposed [demonstrated] (Keyfactor, n.d.; Bordo & Federal Reserve Board, 2025). The primitives exist: NIST ratified FIPS 203/204/205 on 13 August 2024 [deployed] (NIST, 2024), and CNSA 2.0 sets the mandate — PQC-capable acquisitions by January 2027, full resistance by 2035 [target] (NSA, 2022). This is the honest seed of quantum in this paper: PQC-at-archive as archival ethics, plus a solver-interchange schema that keeps optimization vendor-independent over time (NIST crypto-agility, 2025) [roadmap]. Data encrypted with today's classical ciphers and stored for fifty years is a fifty-year countdown already running.

**Decision five — perpetual-wisdom versus project-amnesia.** SMILE Phase 6 makes the retired operator's instinct an institutional asset rather than an exit interview. Case B's tuning did not evaporate at go-live; it became a compounding baseline, the same way China Mobile's L4 NOC use case banked ~30% MTTR and manpower reductions as durable capability [TM Forum-reported] (TM Forum, 2024). Amnesia is the default cost of every project that ships and forgets.

One methodological candor, stated once: SMILE and the DTC maturity spine are both this paper's analytical lenses and, as 2026 formulations, themselves under evaluation — a circularity the reader should weigh, and which the two primary deployments (Case A, Case B) exist to counterbalance with evidence outside the author's own frameworks.

The partition test remains the falsifier. If a sovereign edge cannot keep reality readable when the network dies — as Case A's did and the Iberian grid's could not — this thesis is wrong. Every disaster of 2023–2026 argues it is right, and every procurement signed this year decides which city answers the next one.

## 11. Actionable Annex: What to Sign on Monday

This chapter converts the argument into contract language, a maturity ladder, a scorecard, and a calendar. It restates no motif — it operationalizes them. A procurement officer, a CTO, or a city CIO can lift the clauses below into a live tender. Every number is labelled by evidence tier; nothing here is aspirational unless marked.

### 11.1 The procurement-annex clause set

Paste these six clauses into any tender for a network, a building-management system, or a digital-twin platform. They encode the partition test as an *acceptance criterion*, not a slogan.

**Clause A — Edge-native processing.** *"The Supplier shall demonstrate that sensing, control, and safety-relevant inference execute on local edge nodes and continue to function with the wide-area uplink physically severed. Cloud dependency for real-time control is a defect."* Anchor: Case A held setpoint control through a real grid outage because inference ran room-local, not cloud-bound [deployed].

**Clause B — Degraded-mode sovereignty (the partition test).** *"On uplink loss, the system shall enter a documented degraded mode in which reality remains locally readable and recorded, with automatic reconciliation on reconnection. The Supplier shall evidence one rehearsed failover."* The falsifier is explicit: a system that goes dark or amnesiac on partition fails. Operators already rehearse this — Liberty Caribbean activated Starlink Direct-to-Cell as satellite failover during Hurricane Melissa, restoring coverage to standard phones with no new handset (Liberty Latin America, 2025) [deployed]; AT&T's FirstNet Compact Rapid Deployables restored Helene comms without commercial power (AT&T, 2024) [deployed].

**Clause C — PQC-at-archive with crypto-agility.** *"All data with a confidentiality lifetime beyond 2030 shall be encrypted at rest using a NIST-ratified post-quantum algorithm (FIPS 203/204/205), deployed in hybrid classical+PQC mode, and the Supplier shall evidence the capability to rotate algorithms without system interruption."* Basis: NIST finalized ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA on 13 August 2024, effective immediately (NIST, 2024) [deployed]; the Federal Reserve models "harvest-now-decrypt-later" as a live financial risk (Bordo & Federal Reserve Board, 2025) [peer-reviewed-theory]; NIST defines crypto-agility as in-flight algorithm replacement (NIST, 2025) [roadmap]. CNSA 2.0 sets the deadline that forces the decision now: PQC-capable acquisitions by January 2027 (NSA, 2022) [target].

**Clause D — MIM1 + MIM7 conformance.** *"Interfaces shall conform to MIM Level 1 (shared data models) and MIM Level 7 (open marketplace / vendor-replaceable components). The Supplier shall not lock the Buyer to a proprietary twin format."* This makes reality a boundary object across operator, city, and clinic domains rather than a vendor silo, aligned with OAIS (ISO 14721, CCSDS, 2012) [deployed] and FAIR (Wilkinson et al., 2016) [peer-reviewed-theory].

**Clause E — Data-egress and repatriation rights.** *"The Buyer may export all data and metadata in an open, self-describing format at any time, at no egress fee, and may repatriate to a Buyer-controlled or third-party environment."* This is now law, not preference: the EU Data Act became applicable 12 September 2025 and bans egress fees by January 2027 (European Commission, 2025) [deployed]; Gaia-X (2022) [demonstrated] and IDS (IDSA, n.d.) [demonstrated] supply the reference architecture.

**Clause F — Benchmark disclosure for any quantum or AI claim.** *"Any 'quantum', 'AI-optimized', or 'self-healing' performance claim shall cite the benchmark, baseline, and dataset. Marketing figures without a reproducible baseline are inadmissible."* Enforce the demonstrated-peak-versus-target gap: Rakuten's RIC targets 15–20% RAN-energy reduction nationwide [target] but trials demonstrated up to 25% (Rakuten Mobile & Rakuten Symphony, 2025) [demonstrated] — buyers must know which number they are buying.

### 11.2 L0–L5 self-assessment and the single-use-case-first recipe

Score each capability against TM Forum Autonomous Networks L0 (manual) → L5 (full autonomy). Do **not** attempt whole-portfolio autonomy at once — the proven path is to certify **one** use case, then replicate.

| Level | Test the buyer can run today |
|---|---|
| L0–L1 | Humans read dashboards; no closed loop. |
| L2 | Assisted: system recommends, human executes. |
| L3 | Conditional closed loop in one domain, human sets policy and reviews outcomes. |
| L4 | Self-configuring/optimizing/healing in a **single certified use case**, human out of the review loop. |
| L5 | Cross-domain autonomy across contexts. |

The recipe: pick one high-leverage, measurable loop and drive it toward L4 before touching the next. Rakuten's RAN-energy use case is the template — a nationwide RIC on one certified loop (Rakuten Mobile & Rakuten Symphony, 2025) [deployed]. In single certified use cases, China Mobile reported ~30% lower fault/complaint MTTR and >30% O&M headcount reduction in the NOC, and Telefónica Vivo reported up to 90% route-convergence gains in network planning (TM Forum, 2024) [TM Forum-reported operator self-claim]. The Shibuya MRO case proves the mechanism at the cell edge but sits at **L3, scenario-scoped** (§4.4): CIO tuning lifted problem-cell handover success on the acted relations ~84%→~92% (mean +8.0 pts, peak +11.1) with a human still setting policy [demonstrated] — one loop, one number, replicable, with the L4 gap named.

### 11.3 The "Five Decisions" scorecard

Score your own city or network 0–2 on each (0 = absent, 1 = partial, 2 = contractual). A total below 6 means reality goes dark in the next disaster.

1. **Does control survive partition?** (Clause A/B) — the Iberian partition (§1.1) took comms down *with* the grid because they shared a single dependency [demonstrated]. 0/1/2
2. **Is reality still recorded when the uplink dies?** The Helene backhaul severance (§1.1) kept sites dark after power returned [demonstrated]. 0/1/2
3. **Is long-lived data quantum-safe today?** (Clause C) 0/1/2
4. **Can you leave your vendor with your data intact?** (Clause D/E) 0/1/2
5. **Is one self-healing loop certified and measured?** (Clause F, §11.2) 0/1/2

### 11.4 Sequencing — the calendar

- **Next week:** insert Clauses A–F into your next live tender; run the Five-Decisions scorecard on one critical system.
- **Next year:** certify one use case to L4 (RAN-energy, building-heat, or handover); deploy hybrid PQC on your longest-lived archive ahead of the January 2027 CNSA gate [target].
- **Next five years:** reach MIM7 marketplace conformance so components are swappable; make every degraded-mode failover rehearsed, not theoretical.
- **Next decade:** reality remains sovereign, explainable, and quantum-safe by architecture — so the downstream hospital's patient-telemetry feed downstream still resolves true the hour the network drops, because the decisions locked in this decade made it impossible for it to lie.

The architecture window is open now and closing on a published schedule. Sign accordingly.
